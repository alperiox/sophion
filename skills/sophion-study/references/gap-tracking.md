# Gap Tracking Reference

## What is a Learning Gap?

A learning gap is something you accepted as true without being able to explain **why** it's true. In the trust hierarchy:

- **Verified**: You can explain it from first principles
- **Gap**: You accepted it but can't fully justify it
- **Unknown**: You haven't encountered it yet

## When to Log a Gap

Log a gap when the user:
- Says "ok" or "makes sense" to a complex claim
- Cannot explain a concept when asked
- Gets a prediction/conjecture wrong about how something works
- Skips over a step in reasoning

## Gap Format

Each gap has:
- **Topic**: The broad area (e.g., "diffusion models", "attention mechanisms")
- **Question**: The specific thing they don't understand
- **Status**: "open" (needs work) or "resolved" (understood)
- **Resolution**: Their explanation when they finally understand it

## When to Resolve

Resolve a gap ONLY when the user can:
1. Explain the concept in their own words
2. Connect it to related concepts they already understand
3. Predict consequences or edge cases correctly

Do NOT resolve just because they can recite a definition.
